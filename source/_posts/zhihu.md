## What exactly are we striving for throughout our entire lives?

There are some philosophical perspectives.

Existentialism: Existentialist philosophers emphasize the individual's freedom, responsibility, and the search for meaning in an apparently meaningless world. According to existentialism, life's meaning is not predetermined but is created by each individual through their choices and actions. It suggests that individuals must confront the existential dilemmas of existence, such as the inevitability of death and the absurdity of life, and find their own purpose and values.

Absurdism: Absurdism, influenced by existentialism, acknowledges the inherent contradiction between the human search for meaning and the universe's apparent indifference. Absurdists argue that the search for an ultimate meaning is futile, but individuals can still find personal meaning in defying the absurdity of existence and embracing their freedom to create their own values and purpose.

Hedonism: Hedonism asserts that the pursuit of pleasure and happiness is the ultimate aim of life. It suggests that maximizing personal pleasure, whether through sensual experiences, intellectual pursuits, or emotional fulfillment, is the key to a meaningful life.

Eudaimonism: Eudaimonism, rooted in ancient Greek philosophy, posits that the goal of life is to live virtuously and flourish as a human being. It emphasizes the cultivation of virtues, such as wisdom, courage, and justice, and the fulfillment of one's potential to lead a fulfilling and meaningful life.

Nihilism: Nihilism challenges the notion of inherent meaning in life and asserts that life is fundamentally without objective meaning, value, or purpose. Nihilists argue that individuals must confront the meaninglessness of existence and create their own subjective meaning or embrace a perspective of existential nihilism.

Transcendentalism: Transcendentalist philosophy, popularized by thinkers like Ralph Waldo Emerson and Henry David Thoreau, emphasizes the importance of spiritual and intuitive experience in finding meaning in life. It suggests that individuals can connect with a higher spiritual reality or transcend the limitations of the material world to discover a deeper sense of purpose and harmony.

It's important to note that these philosophical perspectives represent different approaches to the question of life's meaning, and individuals may resonate with different aspects of each perspective or combine multiple viewpoints to form their own understanding. Ultimately, the pursuit of meaning is a deeply personal and subjective endeavor that requires introspection, self-reflection, and the exploration of one's values, passions, and relationships.
